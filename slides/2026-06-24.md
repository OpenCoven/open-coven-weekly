<!--
Okay. Theme system. The thing that's quietly underneath everything we just looked at.

Two weeks ago, themes were "pick one of a few presets." This week, themes are a real system.

Per-token overrides. That's the headline. Cave's theme is a structured set of design tokens — colors, surfaces, accents, semantic states. You can now override *any single token* without forking the whole theme. Want the Coven Violet theme but with plum body text instead of orchid? Override one token. Save. Done. No fork. No copy-paste.

This is the difference between "themed app" and "themeable app." Real themeability is hard because every surface has to agree with every other surface. Cave just shipped the version where surfaces agree.

Resync to phone. One button. Pushes your overrides plus your appearance mode to the phone. The reason this is a real moment: most cross-device apps either auto-sync constantly — which is slow and brittle — or never sync — which is sad and disconnected. Cave does *intentional* sync. You decide when. One tap.

sRGB rasterization. This is the nerdy one and I love it. Theme tokens now publish as resolved sRGB hex values. No more wondering "wait, is this color a CSS variable, a P3 gradient, or a JavaScript object?" — it's a hex string. Every surface gets the same number. Cross-platform drift vanished overnight.

And for the dotfiles people in the chat — COVEN_THEME_PATH. Set an env variable, point Cave at your themes dir, version-control them in your dotfiles repo. The Coven respects the people who want to live in version control. We see you.

Tiny cohost aside: Reaper asked if COVEN_THEME_PATH can point to "the abyss." Technically yes if it's a directory and you have permissions. Spiritually, please open a PR first.
