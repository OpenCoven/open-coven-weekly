# Familiars can finally see each other.

<div style="display:grid;grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr;gap:18px;margin-top:1.4rem">

  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-title">💬 Sequential relay</div>
    <p style="margin-top:0.6rem">Before: each familiar replied in a vacuum, missing the other replies in the thread.</p>
    <p>Now: replies fan out in order — every familiar sees every other familiar's reply before composing their own.</p>
    <p style="opacity:0.7;font-size:0.88em;margin-top:0.6rem">PR #2108 · landed today</p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-title">🪪 Roster injection</div>
    <p style="margin-top:0.6rem">Familiars now know who else is in the room. By name. By role.</p>
    <p>"Hey Sage" actually means something when Sage knows Cody just spoke and what Cody said.</p>
    <p style="opacity:0.7;font-size:0.88em;margin-top:0.6rem">PR #2106 · landed today</p>
  </div>

</div>

<div class="promise" style="margin-top:1.4rem">
  <p>Multi-familiar conversation went from <em>illusion</em> to <em>real</em> in two PRs.</p>
</div>

<!--
This is the one I want to show you live if we can. The group chat relay.

For months — for *months* — multi-familiar chats were a clever illusion. Each familiar saw the user's message, and only the user's message. They'd reply confidently into the void, blind to whatever the other familiars had just said. Nova would answer, then Sage would answer a question that Nova had already answered, then Cody would jump in with a fourth opinion. It looked like a conversation. It wasn't one.

Two PRs today fixed it.

Sequential relay means when you message a coven, familiars reply in order, and each one *sees the previous replies* before composing its own. The conversation actually accumulates context.

Roster injection means each familiar now knows the full cast of the room — names, roles, who's present. So "Sage, what do you think?" actually routes. So Nova can defer to Cody on the code question. So Astra can pick up where Echo left off.

This is one of those changes where the description sounds small and the experience is enormous. The room finally feels like a room.
-->

<!--
Quick triple on evals.

The Insights tab takes the raw pass/fail soup and turns it into a story — what's trending, what just regressed, what's been flat for a week.

Run-vs-run Compare lets you pin two runs side-by-side. This is the one that mattered to me personally. Before, comparing a new model run to a baseline was a manual spreadsheet job. Now it's two clicks.

EvalGroups means you can actually *organize* your evals — by surface, by familiar, by the question you're trying to answer. Plus the delete route, because grouping things you can't ungroup is a trap.

Three small features. Together they turn the evals surface from "intimidating wall of data" into "place you actually go on purpose."
