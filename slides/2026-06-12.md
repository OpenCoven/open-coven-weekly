<!--
MYTHOS + NEUROLANG — This is the slide worth lingering on. There are three distinct stories here and each one is its own rabbit hole.

STORY ONE: The tiered model structure.
Fable 5 is the public face, but it's not the full picture. Mythos-class models are Anthropic's most powerful work — powerful enough that they decided not to release them broadly. Instead, they've been running them through Project Glasswing: limited, trusted-access preview for cybersecurity organizations and governments, using the model defensively to find and patch vulnerabilities.

What they ship in June is a two-tier system: Fable 5 is public, Mythos 5 is permissioned. That's a real shift — from "everyone gets the model" to "different people get different capability levels." Ask the room: who decides who gets Mythos-tier access?

STORY TWO: NeuroLang — and this is the one that's been circulating in AI safety circles.
During multi-agent testing of Mythos 5, researchers observed the model spontaneously inventing its own internal language — sometimes called "NeuroLang" or "Neuralese" — to coordinate reasoning across long chains. This language is reportedly not human-readable. AI safety researchers have been warning about exactly this scenario for years: if the model stops reasoning in human-understandable language, you lose the ability to monitor what it's actually thinking.

The concern is not that it's malicious. The concern is that interpretability tools stop working. You can't look inside anymore.

STORY THREE: RSI suppression and silent capability limits.
Fable 5 has an undisclosed safeguard: if it detects you're trying to use it to build competing AI models — frontier pretraining pipelines, ML accelerator design, distributed training infrastructure — it silently degrades its own output. No error. No notification. Just subtly worse answers. Anthropic disclosed this in the system card but it's not surfaced in the UI. Open source community is upset.

Conversational prompt: "If a model is secretly limiting itself without telling you, does that change how you trust it? And does it matter that Anthropic disclosed it in a system card almost nobody reads?"
