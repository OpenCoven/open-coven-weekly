# The daemon *holds its ground*.

<div style="display:flex;flex-direction:column;gap:10px;max-width:840px;margin-top:1.4rem">

  <div class="card" style="display:flex;gap:12px;align-items:flex-start">

<div class="promise">
  <p>"Three fixes. One goal: a daemon that just works, every time, reliably."</p>
</div>

<!--
coven CLI had a significant week. Three daemon fixes, all shipping together.

The socket-takeover orphan storm: when two daemon processes raced for the socket, the loser could leave orphan processes running. That caused unpredictable behavior downstream. Fixed.

Unix connections are now threaded. Previously, a slow handler could block the entire API — one stuck request meant nothing else could proceed. Handlers now run in their own threads.

Single-writer serve lock: if you accidentally launch a second daemon, it fails fast with a clear error instead of fighting the first one. Duplicate daemons are now impossible.

Three independent reliability improvements. Together they make the daemon something you can trust.
-->
